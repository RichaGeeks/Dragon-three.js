The Art of Dragon Animation

Alright, so picture this: I thought, why not unleash a dragon on my website? Not literally, but as close as it gets in 3D. With Three.js, I’ve created a beast that’s fierce, cool, and, yeah, kinda terrifying—but in the best way. This project started as a fun experiment to see how real and intimidating I could make it look, and it turns out dragons are the ultimate combo of epic and awesome. Every scale, every wing flap it’s all about taking my skills up a notch in 3D web magic!

Dragon Myths Worldwide

Nearly every culture has some form of dragon in its mythology. For example, the Quetzalcoatl of Mesoamerican mythology is a feathered serpent revered as a creator god, while the Nāga in Hindu and Buddhist traditions are serpent-like beings often associated with protection and wisdom. The Myth of the Ouroboros: In some ancient myths, a dragon or serpent is depicted eating its own tail, creating a symbol known as the Ouroboros. This represents infinity, the cycle of life and death, and the concept of eternal renewal.
